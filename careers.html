The Sound of Salsa

The sound of salsa music is energetic and vibrant, full of rhythm and emotion. It uses instruments like congas, bongos, timbals, cowbells, trumpets, and piano to create a powerful blend of sound that feels alive.

According to Joaquin Arteaga, salsa’s rhythm is built on the clave pattern, which is the heartbeat of the music and helps dancers connect their movements to the timing of the beat. The layers of percussion, melody, and vocals all work together to create energy and flow.

Aurora Flores explains that salsa grew out of Afro-Caribbean and Latin roots and found its voice in New York City during the 1960s and 70s. It was shaped by musicians from Cuba, Puerto Rico, and other parts of Latin America who combined traditional rhythms with jazz and modern influences.

Salsa became a symbol of identity and pride for Latino communities, expressing joy, struggle, and resilience. Whether it’s played live or through speakers, salsa brings people together through its rhythm, history, and emotion, celebrating life through sound and movement.
